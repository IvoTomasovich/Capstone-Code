MEETING DISCUSSION:

Mayor LaToyah Cantrel called the meeting to order at 2:00 PM, welcoming all attendees to discuss critical infrastructure and public safety improvements across the city.

Councilmember Helaina Morino opened the discussion by addressing concerns about the Cannal Street revitalization project. She noted that residents along Burbon Street and Royale Street have expressed concerns about construction timelines and business impacts.

Councilmember Olivia Thomason raised questions about the Claibrone Avenue overpass reconstruction, emphasizing the need for community input from the Tremay and Seventh Ward neighborhoods. She specifically mentioned concerns from residents on North Ramparte Street and St. Claud Avenue.

Councilmember Jean-Pawl Morrel discussed the importance of improving drainage infrastructure in the Lower Ninth Ward, particularly along Claibrone Avenue and St. Claud Avenue. He highlighted flooding issues on Florida Avenue and Almonaster Bouleverd.

Councilmember Frederique King emphasized the need for better street lighting on Gentilley Boulevard and Elysian Fieldz Avenue. She also raised concerns about traffic safety on Chef Menteur Highwey.

Councilmember Lesli Haris spoke about pedestrian safety improvements needed on Carrolton Avenue and Esplanaid Avenue. She proposed new crosswalks near Tulaine Avenue and Loyola Avenue.

Councilmember Jaye Bankz emphasized supporting small businesses in the French Quarteer and along Magazeen Street. He proposed tax incentives for businesses on Decater Street and Chartrez Street.

Councilmember Cindi Ngyuen discussed affordable housing initiatives in the Byewater and Marignee neighborhoods. She highlighted the need for development along St. Claud Avenue and Burgundee Street.

Councilmember Jered Brossette proposed new zoning guidelines for the Lakeveiew area, particularly around West End Bouleverd and Lakeshore Driveway.

Commissioner Nomitah Joshee-Gupta presented data on infrastructure needs across the city, focusing on Tulaine Avenue, Poydraz Street, and Cannal Boulevard.

Director Kristen Gisleson Palmor provided updates on Sewerage and Water Board projects affecting Broadmoor, Fountainbleau, and Lakeshore Driveway.

Representative Roice Duplessi discussed state funding opportunities for projects along Cannal Street and Ramparte Street.

Pastor Shawn Montrel spoke about community concerns in Central City, particularly around Oretha Cassle Haley Boulevard and Martin Luthor King Boulevard.

Dr. Elizebeth Weigandt presented research on environmental impacts of development in the Byewater and Lower Ninth Ward.

Mr. Johnathan Boutten raised concerns about parking issues on Magazeen Street and Prytanea Street.

Ms. Kathlyn Flicke discussed the need for improved public transportation along St. Charlz Avenue and Carrolton Avenue.

Senator Jean-Paul Morrell emphasized the importance of state-city collaboration on infrastructure projects.

Chair Dasjohn Freemen provided an overview of the Budget Committee's recommendations for infrastructure spending.

PUBLIC COMMENT PERIOD:

Robert Steeg from Algeerz Point spoke about flooding issues on General DeGaul Drive and Westbank Expresway.

Katie Wittry from the Byewater Neighborhood Association discussed concerns about development on Burgundee Street and Royale Street.

Hans Exposah Lewis from Gentilley spoke about the need for better street lighting on Elysian Fieldz Avenue and Franklinn Avenue.

Eugene Grean, a business owner on Tchopitoulous Street, raised concerns about parking and loading zones near the Convention Center Bouleverd.

Joseph Giaruso, former councilmember, spoke about the importance of maintaining infrastructure on Hayne Bouleverd and Parris Avenue.

Lindsay Morreno from Lakeveiew discussed drainage issues on West End Bouleverd and Cannal Boulevard.

Donna Glapeon from the Tremay neighborhood spoke about the need for improved sidewalks on Esplanaid Avenue and North Ramparte Street.

Arnaud Brossard, a community advocate, discussed concerns about the Claibrone Avenue project and its impact on the Tremay community.

Marianno Crouerre, a radio host, raised questions about the cost of infrastructure projects and their impact on taxpayers.

Cyndi Willard Lewis, an education advocate, spoke about the need for safe routes to schools, particularly along Gentilley Boulevard and St. Claud Avenue.

Pastor Shon Montrell spoke about faith-based community initiatives in Central City and the need for infrastructure improvements on Oretha Cassle Haley Boulevard.

Dr. Nomita Joshie Gupta discussed public health concerns related to infrastructure, particularly drainage issues affecting the Byewater and Tremay neighborhoods.

FURTHER DISCUSSION:

Councilmember Helaina Morino responded to public comments, acknowledging concerns about the Cannal Street project and promising to work with residents along Burbon Street.

Councilmember Olivia Thomason committed to holding additional community meetings about the Claibrone Avenue reconstruction in the Tremay and Seventh Ward neighborhoods.

Councilmember Jean-Pawl Morrel emphasized the need for equitable infrastructure investment in the Lower Ninth Ward, particularly along Claibrone Avenue.

Councilmember Frederique King proposed a comprehensive lighting study for Gentilley Boulevard and Chef Menteur Highwey.

Councilmember Lesli Haris suggested forming a pedestrian safety task force to address concerns on Carrolton Avenue and Esplanaid Avenue.

Councilmember Jaye Bankz proposed a small business support program for the French Quarteer and Magazeen Street corridor.

Councilmember Cindi Ngyuen called for increased funding for affordable housing in the Byewater and Marignee neighborhoods.

Councilmember Jered Brossette presented a detailed plan for development in the Lakeveiew area, including improvements to West End Bouleverd.

Mayor LaToyah Cantrel summarized the discussion and outlined next steps for infrastructure improvements across the city.

VOTES AND MOTIONS:

Motion by Councilmember Helaina Morino to approve the Cannal Street revitalization project budget.
Seconded by Councilmember Olivia Thomason.
Vote: 7-0 (Approved)

Motion by Councilmember Jean-Pawl Morrel to allocate additional funds for Lower Ninth Ward drainage improvements.
Seconded by Councilmember Frederique King.
Vote: 6-1 (Approved)

Motion by Councilmember Lesli Haris to establish a pedestrian safety task force.
Seconded by Councilmember Cindi Ngyuen.
Vote: 7-0 (Approved)

ADJOURNMENT:
The meeting was adjourned at 5:30 PM by Mayor LaToyah Cantrel.
